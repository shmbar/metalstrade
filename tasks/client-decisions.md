# Dashboard & reports — 6 quick decisions

To make every screen show the same, correct figures, we need your call on 6 points.
Each has our recommendation — you can just reply "all recommended" or tell us where you differ.

---

**1. How should the dashboard show PROFIT?**
- **(A) By year (recommended):** profit = everything sold this year − everything spent this year. Material you bought but haven't sold yet does *not* count as a loss. Reads like a normal yearly overview.
- **(B) By deal:** profit per contract — each purchase matched to its own sales. Material bought but not yet sold shows as a loss until it's sold (this is why the current dashboard shows a big negative number).

**2. Should unfinished (draft) invoices count as sales / money owed?**
- **Recommended: No** — only finalized, non-cancelled invoices count. (Please confirm "draft" = an invoice you haven't issued yet.)

**3. When we show how much an invoice still owes, calculate it fresh or trust a saved number?**
- **Recommended: always calculate fresh** = invoice total − payments received. This guarantees a stale saved figure can never show a wrong balance.

**4. How should "overdue" work for invoices with no due date entered?**
- Today an invoice is "overdue" only once its due date has passed. Many invoices have **no due date entered**, so they all show as "balance" and never as "due/overdue" (that's why you see "0 due / 68 balance").
- Pick one: **(A)** require a due/delivery date on invoices, or **(B)** we assume a default payment term (e.g., due **30 days** after the invoice date) when none is entered. *Which do you prefer, and what term?*

**5. Currencies (USD & EUR) — how to combine into one total?**
- For combined figures (like total profit), we convert everything to one base currency.
- **Recommended: base = USD**, using **one company exchange rate** (updated as needed) rather than a rate typed on each contract — per-contract rates sometimes get left blank, which understates totals.
- Confirm base currency = USD, and that a single company rate is OK.

**6. Credit Notes / Final Notes**
- When an invoice is later adjusted by a Credit Note or Final Note, we treat that note as the final value (it replaces the original invoice amount).
- **Recommended: confirm yes** — that's the correct behaviour.

---

*Once we have these, all screens (dashboard, cashflow, contracts, invoices, reports, alerts) will calculate identically and the "wrong numbers" issues are resolved at the source.*
